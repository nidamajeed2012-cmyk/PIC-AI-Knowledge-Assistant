About

About the PIC Programme

PIC refers to the Prevention of Infection Committee, connected to work on infection prevention, antimicrobial resistance, primary care, and public health research.

The programme aims to support prevention-focused approaches, evidence-informed concept notes, and pilot ideas that may reduce avoidable infections and unnecessary antibiotic use. This portfolio helps organise the material so relevant information can be found quickly during research or presentation preparation.

Problem / Opportunity

From scattered documents to usable evidence

The problem is not lack of information. The problem is that the information is spread across many documents and needs to be transformed into practical outputs.

📚

Large literature

AMR and infection prevention literature is extensive and time-consuming to review.

🗂️

Many document types

Reports, papers, meeting notes, concept ideas, screenshots, and proposal materials need structure.

🔎

Need fast retrieval

During concept note writing, it should be easy to find key points, gaps, and supporting evidence.

✨

AI-assisted synthesis

LLMs can help summarise, compare, and convert documents into presentation-ready outputs.

Responsible Use

Limitations and responsible use

AI is useful for organising and summarising, but expert review remains essential.

✓AI outputs need expert review before use in formal public health documents.
✓Document links must be added manually if the HTML file is used outside the original workspace.
✓Evidence around OM-89 and OM-85 should be interpreted carefully and transparently.
✓Confidential or sensitive information should not be uploaded or linked without permission.
✓A chatbot can be added later as a future module once documents are ready and permissions are clear.
